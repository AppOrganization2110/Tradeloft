# Chart Patterns — Reference Guide

## Reversal Patterns

### Head & Shoulders (Bearish)
- **Structure**: left shoulder → head (higher high) → right shoulder (lower high)
- **Neckline**: support connecting the two troughs
- **Confirmation**: close below neckline
- **Target**: measure height from head to neckline, project down from breakout
- **Failed H&S**: price reclaims neckline → aggressive long opportunity
- **Notes**: volume typically declining into right shoulder

### Inverse Head & Shoulders (Bullish)
- Mirror of above — reversal from downtrend
- **Target**: neckline breakout + height of formation
- **Best**: when forms at multi-month support or 200 MA

### Double Top (Bearish)
- Two peaks at approximately the same level
- **Confirmation**: close below the valley between peaks
- **Target**: distance from peak to valley, projected down
- **Stronger signal**: second top lower than first

### Double Bottom (Bullish)
- Two troughs at approximately the same level
- **Confirmation**: close above the peak between troughs
- **W-pattern**: common name; second bottom often slightly higher

### Triple Top / Bottom
- Three attempts at the same level — stronger than double
- Failure rate lower; usually decisive breakout when it comes

### Rounding Bottom (Cup)
- Gradual, smooth rounded base over weeks/months
- Often followed by "handle" (minor pullback) before breakout
- **Cup & Handle**: bullish continuation — target = depth of cup added to breakout

## Continuation Patterns

### Bull Flag
- **Structure**: sharp impulse move up → tight rectangular consolidation (downward slope)
- **Entry**: breakout above flag upper boundary
- **Target**: measure the flagpole (impulse), add to breakout
- **Best**: high volume on pole, low volume in flag, expanding volume on breakout

### Bear Flag
- Mirror: sharp impulse down → tight rectangular consolidation (upward slope)
- Entry on breakdown below flag lower boundary

### Bull Pennant
- Like bull flag but consolidation forms converging trendlines (triangle shape)
- Shorter duration than flags; equally reliable

### Ascending Triangle
- Flat resistance top + rising lows
- **Bias**: bullish (buyers getting more aggressive)
- **Entry**: breakout above flat resistance
- **Can fail**: break below rising support = strong reversal signal

### Descending Triangle
- Flat support bottom + declining highs
- **Bias**: bearish (sellers getting more aggressive)
- **Entry**: breakdown below flat support

### Symmetrical Triangle
- Converging highs and lows — coil of energy
- **No directional bias** by itself — break direction is the signal
- **Measured target**: height of widest part of triangle

### Wedge (Rising)
- Both highs and lows rising, but upper trendline rising faster
- **Bearish** pattern despite upward price movement
- **Entry**: break below lower trendline

### Wedge (Falling)
- Both highs and lows falling, lower trendline steeper
- **Bullish** pattern despite downward price movement
- **Entry**: break above upper trendline

## Candlestick Patterns (High-Value)

### Single-Candle Reversals
| Pattern | Type | Meaning |
|---------|------|---------|
| Hammer | Bullish | Long lower wick at support — buyers absorbed selling |
| Shooting Star | Bearish | Long upper wick at resistance — rejection |
| Doji | Neutral | Indecision — context matters |
| Marubozu | Trend | Full body candle, no wicks — strong directional momentum |
| Pin Bar | Reversal | Hammer/SS variant — high-probability reversal at key levels |

### Multi-Candle Patterns
| Pattern | Type | Meaning |
|---------|------|---------|
| Engulfing (Bullish) | Reversal | Large green candle fully engulfs prior red |
| Engulfing (Bearish) | Reversal | Large red candle fully engulfs prior green |
| Morning Star | Bullish | 3-candle reversal: big red → small body → big green |
| Evening Star | Bearish | Mirror of morning star |
| Three White Soldiers | Continuation | 3 large green candles — strong buying |
| Three Black Crows | Continuation | 3 large red candles — strong selling |
| Inside Bar | Breakout Setup | Candle within prior range — compression |

## Pattern Reliability Notes

- **Higher timeframe = more reliable** (daily > 4H > 1H > 15m)
- **Context matters**: a hammer at the 200 MA > a hammer at a random price
- **Volume confirmation**: breakouts with volume > breakouts without
- **Failed patterns**: when a pattern fails, the move in the opposite direction is often violent — trade the failure
- **Measured targets**: mechanical targets from patterns are minimums, not maximums
